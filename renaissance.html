The Renaissance

This section explores the cultural, intellectual, and artistic rebirth that swept across Europe from the 14th to 17th centuries. Topics include the rediscovery of classical learning, innovations in art and science, and the beginning of modern humanism.

  • Humanism: Revival of classical texts and the human-centered worldview.
  • Renaissance Art: Techniques, artists, and iconic works from Italy and beyond.
  • Scientific Advancements: Astronomy, anatomy, and the scientific method.
  • Political Thought: Machiavelli, governance, and the rise of secular states.
  • The Printing Press: Gutenberg, book production, and literacy growth.
  • Age of Exploration: Voyages of discovery, new trade routes, and colonial contact.
  • Women in the Renaissance: Education, art, and social roles.
  • Reformation Prelude: Early religious dissent, criticism of the Church.

Each subpage offers access to scanned documents, historical commentary, and curated primary source excerpts for further study.
