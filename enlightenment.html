The Enlightenment

The Enlightenment was an intellectual and cultural movement of the 17th and 18th centuries that emphasized reason, individualism, and skepticism of traditional authority. It played a key role in shaping modern political and social thought.

  • Major Thinkers: Locke, Rousseau, Voltaire, Montesquieu, and others.
  • Political Theory: Social contract, separation of powers, and ideas of liberty.
  • Science & Reason: Rationalism, empiricism, and the scientific method.
  • Religion & Secularism: Deism, critiques of organized religion, and freedom of conscience.

  • Global Impact: Influence on revolutions, reforms, and the rise of modern democracies.
  • Women in the Enlightenment: Education, advocacy, and contributions by figures like Mary Wollstonecraft.
