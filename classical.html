Classical Antiquity

This section explores the civilizations that shaped the classical era, particularly in the Mediterranean and Asia. Political thought, architecture, philosophy, and art flourished during this period, laying the groundwork for much of modern civilization.

  • Ancient Greece: Philosophy, democracy, mythology, city-states.
  • Roman Empire: Law, engineering, military strategy, republic and imperial rule.
  • Persian Empire: Administration, Zoroastrianism, and cultural exchange.
  • Indian Dynasties: Maurya and Gupta contributions to science, mathematics, and governance.
  • Han China: Confucianism, bureaucracy, Silk Road interactions.
  • Culture & Society: Theaters, public forums, civic duties, gender roles, and festivals.
  • Philosophy & Science: Thinkers like Socrates, Plato, Aristotle, and early natural scientists.
  • Art & Architecture: Temples, sculpture, literature, and classical aesthetics.
  • Military Conflicts: The Persian Wars, Punic Wars, Peloponnesian War, and Roman conquests.

Explore these themes through primary texts, translated documents, and archaeological evidence curated within the archive.
