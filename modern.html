Modern Era

This section covers the transformative period from the late 18th century through the early 20th century, characterized by revolutions, industrialization, and global empires.

  • American Revolution: Independence, constitutional governance, and Enlightenment ideals.
  • French Revolution: Radical political change, the rise and fall of Napoleon.
  • Industrial Revolution: Mechanization, factory systems, and labor shifts.
  • Nation Building: Unification of Germany and Italy, modern nationalism.
  • Colonialism: European empires in Asia, Africa, and the Americas.
  • Social Movements: Abolition, suffrage, labor reforms, and education.
  • Science & Technology: Major breakthroughs, inventions, and the rise of modern physics and medicine.
  • World War I: Causes, trench warfare, global alliances, and consequences.

Each section includes original documents, scholarly essays, and visual aids to support a deeper understanding of this critical period in world history.
