Exploring the Prehistoric World

This section explores human activity and development before recorded history, focusing on early tools, art, shelter, and burial practices. Evolutionary theory is intentionally excluded from this presentation.

  • Stone Tools: Types, usage, and discovery sites.
  • Cave Art & Symbolism: Petroglyphs and early expressions of culture.
  • Domestication: Early agricultural and animal-husbandry techniques.
  • Burial Practices: Evidence of spiritual or ritual behavior.
  • Structures & Shelter: Dwellings and primitive construction techniques.
  • Tools & Weapons: Non-stone technologies and materials.
  • Ceremonial Life: Ritual artifacts and sacred sites.
  • Migration Paths: Human movement and settlement routes.
  • Clothing & Adornment: Primitive textiles, beads, and dyes.
  • Trade & Exchange: Evidence of early economic interaction.

Navigate through these topics using the archive's resources and translated texts from archaeological reports and scholarly summaries.
