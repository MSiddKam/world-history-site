Sahih al-Bukhari is a highly revered collection of hadiths (sayings and actions of the Prophet Muhammad ï·º) compiled by the Islamic scholar Imam Muhammad ibn Ismail al-Bukhari in the 9th century CE. Considered the most authentic hadith collection in Sunni Islam, it contains around 7,275 narrations (including repetitions), selected from over 600,000 reports after rigorous evaluation of their chains of transmission and the reliability of narrators. The book is organized by topic, covering matters of faith, law, ethics, and daily life, and it serves as a foundational source for Islamic theology and jurisprudence alongside the Qur'an.

Sahih Muslim is a renowned hadith collection compiled by Imam Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj (Student of Imam Bukhari) (d. 875 CE), consisting of thousands of authentic narrations detailing the sayings, actions, and approvals of the Prophet Muhammad ï·º. Carefully arranged by topic, the collection covers core areas of Islamic belief, worship, ethics, and legal rulings. Imam Muslim applied a meticulous method of verifying narrators and chains of transmission, preserving variant versions of hadiths within grouped sections to highlight subtle differences and strengthen reliability. The result is a clear, structured compilation that has served as a major source of Islamic guidance and scholarship.

Bulugh al-Maram

ðŸ“˜ Full Text

Bulugh al-Maram is a concise hadith collection compiled by Ibn Hajar al-Asqalani (d. 1449 CE) focusing on evidences related to Islamic rulings. It contains over 1,300 hadiths organized under categories like purification, prayer, fasting, zakat, and transactions, each selected to support legal principles in Islamic jurisprudence. The compiler often includes references to where each hadith is found and provides notes on different versions or opinions about authenticity. The work is especially valued for its clarity, brevity, and practical use in the study of Islamic law.
