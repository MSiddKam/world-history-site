Contemporary World History

This section explores the dynamic and interconnected world from the end of World War II to the present, focusing on globalization, technological change, political shifts, and cultural evolution.

  • Cold War: Superpower rivalry, proxy wars, and nuclear tension.
  • Decolonization: Independence movements in Asia, Africa, and the Caribbean.
  • Technological Revolution: Computers, internet, space exploration, and biotech advances.
  • Globalization: Trade, migration, culture, and the world economy.
  • Recent Conflicts: Middle East, Balkans, Afghanistan, and Ukraine.
  • Social Movements: Civil rights, feminism, LGBTQ+, climate activism.
  • Pandemics: HIV/AIDS, SARS, COVID-19 and their global effects.
  • 21st Century Politics: Shifting ideologies, populism, and democracy challenges.

Access a variety of digital archives, government documents, oral histories, and multimedia sources for each topic.
