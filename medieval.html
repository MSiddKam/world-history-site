Medieval History

This section covers the complex developments in societies, religions, economies, and power structures from the fall of Rome to the dawn of the Renaissance across Europe, Asia, and Africa.

  • Early Medieval Period: Collapse of Rome, Germanic kingdoms, and Christianization.
  • Byzantine Empire: Continuation of Roman traditions and Orthodox Christianity.
  • Frankish & Carolingian Realms: Charlemagne and the Holy Roman Empire.
  • Feudalism: Social hierarchy, manorialism, and local governance.
  • The Crusades: Religious wars and cultural contact with the Islamic world.
  • Culture & Religion: Monastic life, cathedral building, scholasticism, and daily life.
  • Trade & Towns: Market development, guilds, and urbanization.
  • Medieval Africa & Asia: Ghana, Mali, Songhai, Tang & Song dynasties, and Mongol expansion.
  • Black Death: Plague, societal shifts, and demographic impact.

These pages include curated texts, maps, and primary sources from medieval chronicles and legal codes for research and education.
